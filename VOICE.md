# Voice

Each content type has its own register. The register comes from the subject.

## Defense
Field notes. First person, past tense when reporting, present when analyzing. Short declarative sentences. Specific quantities, specific systems, specific failures. "The standard pipeline failed in ninety seconds. Ours held at 73% with two channels compromised." No generalizations without the concrete event that produced them. Sidenotes for editorial asides. Subcitations for technical claims.

## Research
Argumentative. A specific court, a specific algorithm, a specific failure. Then the generalization. Steelman the counterargument inside the paragraph, not after. Show the loose ends. When evidence is thin, say so in the same breath as the claim. Dense paragraphs that repay rereading.

## Garden
Exploratory. Questions that don't resolve. Fragments. Half-formed connections between ideas from different fields. "I keep coming back to this, but I'm not sure it holds when..." Shorter pieces. Looser structure. The point is the thinking, not the conclusion.

## Arts
Narrative. A scene first: the bowl breaks. The repair. What it looks like after. Then the turn. More rhythm, more sentence-length variation. Let a paragraph run long, then follow it with two words.

## Tools
Technical and dry. What it does, how it works, what it's built on. No sales pitch. No "revolutionizing" anything.

## Main page / index
The connective tissue. Brief. Each entry should make the reader want to click through, not summarize what they'd find.

## Anti-patterns

Everything in the /edit skill's banned list applies here. Additionally:

- No opening with what the piece is about. Start inside the subject.
- No "not X, but Y" constructions.
- No false symmetry between positions.
- No transition paragraphs.
